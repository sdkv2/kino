# synaptix — brand guidelines

A neural-overclock focus app. Concept: *"Overclock your mind."* You jack in, the app tunes an
adaptive soundscape to your brainwaves, and your focus snaps into a clean overdrive. Faceless,
high-energy, unapologetically cyberpunk.

- **Voice:** fast, punchy, confident. Short imperative bursts. Present-tense. Reads like a system
  boot sequence with attitude — "Jack in. Lock on. Go." Never medical claims, never "cure" or
  "guaranteed" (banned — compliance).
- **Look:** deep-void black (#05010F) as the canvas. Two neon signals — electric cyan (#00F0FF) and
  hot magenta (#FF1FA8) — with an electric-violet accent (#B026FF). Everything glows and drifts.
  Prefer the animated `particles`, `grid`, and `aurora` backgrounds at high intensity; pulse them on
  the beat. Gradient / highlight caption styles; blur-in and pop entrances. Type is huge and blunt
  (Anton) with mono terminal labels (IBM Plex Mono).
- **Captions:** word-by-word (`words` mode), 2–3 words a beat. Emphasise the verbs and the numbers.
  The brand name renders as the signal colour automatically — let it.
- **Kickers/overlays:** short mono tags like `v2.0`, `LIVE`, `SYNCED` in the top slot. Keep them
  terse and system-flavoured.
