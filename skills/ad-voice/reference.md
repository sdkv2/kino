# Ad voice — reference

Companion detail for `SKILL.md`. Read when drafting brand Tone/Voice or when a slop pass needs examples.

## Sources distilled (patterns, not citations)

Short-form / Reels / TikTok creative research (2025–2026) converges on:

- Native > polished. Formal tone in second 0–1 signals "ad" → scroll.
- Hook window ~1.5–3s. Pattern interrupt (visual, audio, or contrarian line).
- Conversational VO; captions for silent / accessibility.
- Specific CTA verbs beat generic (`Try free` > `Learn more`).
- Frameworks that keep winning: PAS, Hook→Demo→Proof→CTA, BAB.

Anti-slop research converges on: ban high-frequency LLM adjectives/verbs; show with numbers; few-shot on-brand examples beat adjective lists ("witty, professional").

## Brand Tone / Voice — filled example

```markdown
## Tone / Voice

- **Register:** plain — closer to a sharp coworker than a TikTok hype house
- **Person:** you
- **Pace:** punchy — one breath per beat; no subordinate-clause stacks
- **Energy:** medium — calm certainty, not shouty
- **Proof style:** specific numbers (time saved, match %, steps)
- **CTA style:** direct
- **Say like this:**
  - "Paste the job post. We'll rebuild the bullets that actually match."
  - "Six seconds. That's how long they look. Make them count."
  - "Same CV everywhere is why you're hearing nothing."
- **Never say like this:**
  - "Unlock your career potential with our innovative platform."
  - "We're passionate about empowering job seekers."
  - "In today's competitive landscape, you need an edge."
- **Banned (brand):** passionate, journey, dream job, hack your career
- **Preferred words:** match, paste, rebuild, callbacks, bullets, listing
```

## Register cheat sheet

| Register | Sounds like | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| casual | friend texting a tip; fragments OK | corporate hedging, "solutions" |
| plain | clear coworker; short full sentences | slang piles, meme-speak |
| sharp | opinionated; contrarian OK | cruelty, punching down |
| warm | supportive; still specific | therapy-speak, "you've got this" filler |
| dry | understated; irony light | forced jokes, exclamation spam |

## Hook formulas (fill with product truth)

1. **Pain named** — `Still [specific bad habit]?`
2. **Cost** — `[Bad process] is costing you [time/money/outcome].`
3. **Contrarian** — `Your [sacred cow] isn't [common diagnosis]. It's [reframe].`
4. **Result** — `[Specific outcome] after [small action].`
5. **Demo pull** — `Watch what happens when you [action].`
6. **Stop** — `Stop [common action] if you want [outcome].`
7. **Number** — `[N] [things] your [artifact] is missing.`
8. **Before flash** — `I used to [old way]. Then [trigger].`

Test 3–5 hooks against the same body when optimizing; don't rewrite the whole trailer each time.

## PAS beat map (~20s)

| Beat | Job | Copy job |
|---|---|---|
| 0 opener | Hook | Name pain or pattern interrupt — no brand |
| 1–3 app/motion | Agitate + show | Footage carries; VO twists the knife once |
| 4–5 | Solution | What changes when they use the product |
| 6 CTA | Action | Brand + URL + concrete verb |

## Caption vs VO

| VO (spoken) | Caption (scrolled) |
|---|---|
| Can be a full short sentence | 2–6 words when possible |
| Carries reasoning | Carries the claim |
| May include soft asides | Never a paragraph |

If caption ≈ full VO, you're dumping. Trim caption to the spine.

## Slop cluster example

Bad (machine-default):

> Seamlessly unlock the power of AI to effortlessly transform your workflow and take it to the next level.

Good (defensible):

> Paste a listing. Get bullets that match it — about twenty minutes, start to finish.

## Extra soft-bans (use sparingly; kill if clustered)

`journey` · `passion` / `passionate` · `delightful` · `magical` · `literally` (as intensifier) ·
`honestly` / `game changer` · `secret` / `hack` (unless brand owns that voice) ·
`I was struggling with…` · `That's when I found…` · `As a [role], I know…` (fake-UGC openers)

## Brand section checklist

When filling or reviewing Tone / Voice:

- [ ] Register + person + pace + energy set (one value each)
- [ ] ≥2 Say-like-this lines with real product nouns
- [ ] ≥2 Never-say-like-this lines (same idea, wrong voice)
- [ ] Brand banned list non-empty OR explicit "none beyond house list"
- [ ] Preferred words include verbs the product UI actually uses
- [ ] CTA style matches how the brand asks for installs (direct vs soft)
